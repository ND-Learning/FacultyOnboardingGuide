## What ODL is
The Office of Digital Learning (ODL), part of ND Learning at the University of Notre Dame, partners
with faculty to design and build high-quality online, hybrid, bisynchronous, and immersive learning
experiences. A project is run by a Project Manager and supported by Learning Design and Academic
Media teams. Faculty are the Subject-Matter Experts; ODL provides the design, media, and project
management.

## Kinds of projects ODL supports
- **New Course Creation** — a full online or hybrid course built from scratch.
- **Bisynchronous Course** — a blend of synchronous (live) and asynchronous learning.
- **Flipped Classroom** — content delivered online; active learning in class.
- **AI Training / Tool** — incorporating AI tools and literacy into the curriculum.
- **XR / Immersive** — VR, AR, 360 video, or interactive simulations.
- **Single Module** — one focused digital learning unit or resource.

Projects are sized Small / Medium / Large / Extra-Large depending on the number of modules, videos,
and the complexity. Faculty can see the timeline and effort for each combination in the interactive
guide.

## The ODL process — the 7 phases
For each phase: what happens, what ODL handles, what YOU (faculty) do, and the artifacts produced.

### 1. Intake & Discovery
- **What happens:** We review your idea, evaluate scope and feasibility, and align on pedagogical goals.
- **ODL handles:** reviews the intake form, assesses feasibility, drafts the Project Charter and IP agreement.
- **You do:** share your vision, your learners, and the outcomes you want.
- **Artifacts:** Intake Form, Draft Project Charter.

### 2. Design — Course Map
- **What happens:** we set course and learning objectives, decide content, and finalize the Course Map (the blueprint).
- **ODL handles:** leads design conversations, builds the Course Map, plans assessments and the media asset list.
- **You do:** act as the Subject-Matter Expert — lead the learning objectives and content mapping. This is your heaviest design-thinking phase.
- **Artifacts:** Course Map, Design Document, Media Asset List.

### 3. Pre-Production — Scripting
- **What happens:** scripting (Draft → Review → Revise → Finalize), plus filming logistics, locations, consent forms.
- **ODL handles:** guides scripting sessions, builds the production schedule, secures locations, finalizes branding.
- **You do:** draft and review scripts for your videos. Time invested here is the highest-leverage faculty work — it makes filming smooth.
- **Artifacts:** Finalized Scripts, Production Schedule, Media Consent Form.

### 4. Production — Filming
- **What happens:** studio filming, on-location shoots, and B-roll capture.
- **ODL handles:** runs the shoot — direction, lighting, audio, camera — and keeps you prepared on camera.
- **You do:** you're the on-camera talent. Attend filming sessions, deliver content clearly, ensure accuracy.
- **Artifacts:** Raw Footage.

### 5. Post-Production & Build
- **What happens:** we edit footage, create graphics/animation, and build the course in Canvas (Rough Cut 1 → Rough Cut 2 → Final Cut).
- **ODL handles:** edits media, produces graphics/animations, assembles the course site and assessments.
- **You do:** review draft videos and pages for accuracy and pedagogy; build supplementary materials (quizzes, assignments).
- **Artifacts:** Edited Videos, Graphics, Course Site (Canvas).

### 6. QA & Launch
- **What happens:** rigorous quality assurance — accessibility (WCAG), content proofing — then the course goes live.
- **ODL handles:** runs accessibility and QA checks, fixes issues, launches on the LMS.
- **You do:** a full walkthrough and final approval to go live.
- **Artifacts:** Live Course, QA Sign-off.

### 7. Evaluation & Iteration (ongoing)
- **What happens:** after launch, we gather feedback and performance data and plan improvements.
- **ODL handles:** sends the reflection survey, builds the report, runs the retrospective.
- **You do:** review feedback and metrics; reflect and plan future enhancements.
- **Artifacts:** Evaluation Report.

## How long does it take / how much of my time? (route to the guide)
Timelines and effort depend on project type and size. A typical project runs on the order of a few
months; larger courses run longer. For a specific estimate of the **calendar timeline**, the **ODL
team effort**, and **your own faculty hours per phase**, use the interactive Faculty Onboarding Guide
and select your project type and size. *(Pria: link the faculty member to the guide; do not state a
number yourself.)* All figures there are **planning estimates, not commitments** — final scope,
timeline, and budget are set in the Project Charter and approved by the ODL Director.

## Frequently asked questions
- **How do I start a project with ODL?** Submit the intake form ("Let's Work Together" on the ND
  Learning site) or email online@nd.edu. ODL reviews feasibility in the Discovery phase.
- **What's expected of me as faculty?** You're the Subject-Matter Expert: shape objectives and the
  Course Map (Design), draft/review scripts (Pre-Production), appear on camera (Production), review
  edits and build assessments (Post-Production), and give final approval (QA). See each phase above.
- **What does ODL provide?** Learning design, academic media production, and project management,
  generally in-kind. Stipends/software/travel are handled per the Project Charter.
- **What's the heaviest phase for me?** Design (content mapping) and Pre-Production (scripting).
- **Can I add more videos/modules later?** Changes go through ODL's intake/change process and are
  confirmed in the Project Charter — talk to your Project Manager; out-of-scope requests are routed
  to the ODL Director.
- **Is the course accessible?** Yes — all materials are built to WCAG accessibility standards.
- **What tools are used?** Google Workspace, Asana, Canvas (LMS), Frame.io (video review), Panopto,
  Zoom, and Qualtrics (evaluation).
