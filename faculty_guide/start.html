Let's build your course media — together.

Thinking about videos, an online course, or something immersive? Here's the honest picture of what partnering with ODL involves, and how much of your time it really takes.

Your time commitment
A few hours a week.
ODL's team does the heavy lifting — design, filming, editing, and project management. Your role is the ideas and the expertise. In a typical week that looks like:
4–6 hrs / week
For a full online or blended course
2–4 hrs / week
For a single module, or a focused AI or immersive project

Some weeks are heavier — filming and reviewing your videos take a bit more — and many weeks are lighter. Work spreads comfortably across the project, which usually runs over a semester or two for a course, or a few weeks to a couple of months for a smaller piece. These are ODL's planning estimates; we'll pin down the real numbers for your project together.

The whole process, in plain terms

What you actually do

Three simple phases. No technical or video skills needed — that's our job.

1

Shape it

Share your vision and learning goals. We sit down with you and turn them into a course map — the one-page blueprint everyone builds from.

A few hours, up front
2

Record it

You provide the content and, where there's video, step in front of the camera. Roughly ~1 hour to script and ~1 hour to film per video — we handle everything around it.

Spread across a few sessions
3

Review & launch

Watch the drafts, tell us what to tweak, and approve the final. We build it in Canvas, make it accessible, and hand you a finished course.

Quick answers

A few terms you'll hear

Tap to expand. (You don't need to know any of this to start — we'll walk you through it.)

You are the subject-matter expert (SME) — the one who knows the content. You bring the academic expertise and appear on camera where needed; ODL brings the instructional design, production, and project management. No technical, design, or video skills required.
A simple, one-page outline of your course: the modules, what each one should teach, and where video or interactive pieces fit. We build it with you early on, and it becomes the blueprint the whole team works from — so there are no surprises later.
Studio lectures (you, on camera) · lightboard (writing on illuminated glass facing the viewer) · screencasts (narrated screen recordings) · explainer animations & infographics · 360° / VR immersive scenes. We'll recommend the right mix for your goals and budget.
